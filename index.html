Australian Population Growth

Growth Rate In Different Population Densities

The growth rate in Australia is steadily increasing.

However, significantly more people are moving into high density areas and out of low density areas.

This can be visualised by the positive correlation between population and percentage growth rate.
This issue is particularly severe in Victoria but not as present in Queensland.

Growth In Australian Regions


The only year where people where moving out of the cities was 2021. This was the most evident in Melbourne.

Throughout every other year, people were moving away from the regional districts and into city centres.

Use the year slider to see the growth rate of each region over different years.
Use the zoom slider to zoom out. Note the stark differences between city and regional growth.

Focus on different city centres accross Australia.

New Buildings Being Constructed In Australia

Australia is both growing quicker and moving into denser areas.

However, the rate at which new buildings are constructed is decreasing!

The largest housing developer used to be NSW but it has significantly fallen behind the other states (shown on the right).


Contributors To Australian Growth


Australian Growth is primarily lead by immigration followed by natural growth (births and deaths).

This caused the growth rate to significantly reduce during the Covid-19 Pandemic. However, the growth rate has quickly rebounded in the last few years.

Most Migration occurs from parts of Asia due to its closer proximity. Howevver, there are also a significant number of people coming from Colombia and Ireland.
